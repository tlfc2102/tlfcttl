Mumbai

Mumbai Formerly called as Bombay. It is a densely populated city which is located on the west coast of India. the buildngs are a mix of iconic architecture that are part of the old-world, high-rises and include the cultural and traditional structures. The special attractions in Mumbai are the Marine Drive, Gateway of India, Siddhivinayak Temple, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya Museum, Ancient Caves on Elephanta Island and other various places.

The Chhatrapathi Shivaji International Airport is the main commercial airport of Mumbai. In the past, there were two airports in the city, namely: the Santa Cruz Domestic Airport and the Sahar International Airport.

These two airports were fused into one, which is now regarded as the Chhatrapathi Shivaji International Airport. Some refer to the Domestic Terminal as the Santa Cruz Airport and the International Terminal as the Sahar Airport, so they are sometimes seen as two separate airports.

Juhu Beach - 22 minutes via Vaikunthlal Mehta Rd

It is one of the oldest beaches in Mumbai where tourist, as well as, locals spends a good time to beat off the scorching heat. You can reach Juhu beach within 9 minutes from Airport via cab and within 22 minutes via bus. The place is having its own charm including food stalls, blaring music and cheerful faces around you. One can visit nearby ISKON temple which is only a few meters away from the beach. Enjoy Horse riding, buy attractive items from local vendors, and have a colorful chuski while taking a serene walk on the banks of the beach.

Bandra-Worli Sea Link- 26 minutes via Western Express Highway

Bandra Worli is a famous bridge in Mumbai which was opened in 2009. It links Mumbai's suburbs to South Mumbai and that's why the name Bandra-Worli Sea Link is given to it. One must see this bridge to know how beautifully Bandra-Worli Bridge is crafted by an engineer that indicates a flawless masterpiece providing an amazing view of Mumbai and the Indian Ocean. Hire a taxi to reach Bandra-Worli Sea Link within 7 minutes and click some fantastic photographs as a memory of this engineering marvel.

Bandra Fort-39 minutes via Western Express Highway

Bandra Fort was designed by the Portuguese on the shores of Bandra Lands end. This is one of the ultimate places to enjoy the scenic views of the Arabian Sea. This prestigious place overlooks Mahim Bay to the south, the Arabia Sea to the west, and the town of Mahim to the southwest. One can visit Bandra Fort to experience utmost peace and enchanting view of Bandra Sea link on one side and skylines of Mumbai on the other side. This fort is mostly visited by young couples to spend quality time with each other. Hire a cab to reach Bandra fort within 9 minutes from Mumbai Airport.

Borivali National Park-36 minutes via Western Express Highway

Borivali National Park is also named as Sanjay Gandhi National Park which is only 19 minutes away from Mumbai Airport if you drive through a car. However, you can reach this place via local transport easily which may take up to 36 minutes. You will have buy tickets to enter the National Park which is opened from 7:00 AM-6:30 PM. This is one of the favorite picnic spots for most of the tourist where you will see a variety of animals & birds like Lions, Boars, Flying fox, Sunbirds, and others.

But, do visit these places only if you have enough time to take your next flight. It can be very frustrating to wait for your next flight and therefore touring these places will be the best time during your itinerary.
