Sydney

It is one of Australia's largest cities. The Sydney Opera House which is widely known is located at the harbourfront designed like a distinctive sail-like design. This lively city is known for its superb beaches such as Bondi Beach, yach-studded harbour and the Opera House with a towering sail structure. The famous attractions of Sydney are the Sydney Opera House, Sydney Harbour Bridge, Queen Victoria Building, Sydney Tower Eye, beaches of Sydney, Taronga Zoo and other more appealing attractions.

Sydney Kingsford Smith International Airport is Australia's busiest airport, the oldest continually operating commercial airport in the world, and the main gateway to Australia. It is located on the northern shores of Botany Bay.

Sydney Opera House

One of the most recognised buildings in the world, the Sydney Opera House is located in Circular Quay, just 23 kilometres from Sydney Airport. A multi-purpose venue, the Opera House is not just an architectural wonder, but is also home to some of Sydney's most prominent performing arts companies and welcomes a plethora of local and international guests to its stages. Whether you prefer pop music, ballet, opera, dance, contemporary theatre, comedy, or simply wish to marvel at the groundbreaking architecture, there's something for everyone to enjoy at the Sydney Opera House. Share shuttles from Sydney Airport to the Sydney Opera House start from AUD$24 and private airport transfers start from AUD$55.

Bondi Beach

Just over 22 kilometres from Sydney Airport, Bondi Beach is one of Sydney's most sought after destinations, delighting visitors with great surf, gorgeous white sand, patrolled swimming and an abundance of trendy cafés and restaurants. Jog along one of Sydney's most iconic coastal walking trails, the Bondi to Coogee walk, or enjoy a meal and drink at the famous Bondi Icebergs. Share shuttles from Sydney Airport to Bondi Beach start from AUD$48 and private airport transfers start from AUD$72.

SEA LIFE Sydney Aquarium

Less than 19 kilometres from Sydney Airport and located in the popular district of Darling Harbour, SEALIFE Aquarium is one of the world's largest aquariums and a popular tourist attraction in Sydney, especially for families. There are 12 themed zones to explore, including Jurassic Seas, Bay of Rays, and Dugong Island, plus Shark Valley; an ocean tunnel where you can see the creatures of the sea swim right above you. Share shuttles from Sydney Airport to Bondi Beach start from AUD$30 and private airport transfers start from AUD$50.

Sydney Harbour Bridge

Almost 23 kilometres from Sydney Airport, the Sydney Harbour Bridge is the largest steel arch bridge in the world, and one of Sydney's most recognisable icons, spanning the harbour from the CBD to the North Shore. For those wanting a special experience, you can book the BridgeClimb and see the city from 134 metres above the harbour as you summit the height of the bridge, or simply walk across it on the pedestrian walkway to enjoy great views, too. Share shuttles from Sydney Airport to the Sydney Harbour Bridge start from AUD$24 and private airport transfers start from AUD$55.

Taronga Zoo

Located 26 kilometres from Sydney Airport, the award-winning Taronga Zoo is one of Sydney's most famous attractions. Home to more than 4,000 animals, including plenty of Australian wildlife, Taronga Zoo is in a prime location overlooking the beautiful harbour. See the daily seal show, come face-to-face with Australian and exotic birds in the Free-Flight Bird Show, say hello to elephants, giraffes and tigers, and go for a ride on Sydney's only cable car, which traverses over the top of the animal enclosures for a unique bird's-eye view. Share shuttles from Sydney Airport to Taronga Zoo start from AUD$45 and private airport transfers start from AUD$79.
